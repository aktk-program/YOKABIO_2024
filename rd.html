Result & Discussion

Optimization of structure formation conditions

Optimization of structure formation conditions for the formation of DNA origami structures increases time and collection efficiency. We first searched for optimal structure formation conditions using a simple structure without a closing mechanism. We performed experiments for optimization by changing the salt concentration and annealing conditions. For different salt concentrations, we first confirmed formation at different concentrations of Mg²⁺ (12.5 mM~25 mM, in 2.5 mM increments). Since the charge was possibly too strong at these concentrations, Na⁺ 100 mM was added here to study the conditions. In addition, to explore lower salt concentrations, experiments were conducted using Na⁺ only (0.5 M~2.5 M, in 0.25 M increments). The specific salt concentrations used in the experiments were the values in Tables 1 through 3. In addition, an annealing protocol of 2 hours was initially employed; it was placed at 95 °C for 3 minutes and then lowered by 0.6 °C every minute from 95 °C to 20 °C.
After annealing, the different salt concentrations were compared by agarose electrophoresis. Electrophoresis was performed in a cold room at 50 V, 120 min, 4 °C. The results of the electrophoresis are shown in figure 1(a)~(c) below.

Then, observation of sample with Mg²⁺ 22.5 mM and Na 100 mM was performed, resulting in a picture represented in figure 4.

Figure 4. TEM picture of sample with Mg²⁺ 22.5 mM, Na⁺ 100 mM The camera captures a structure of about 50 nm in size.


The structure was observed after purification of gel filtration method. In the annealing method of protocol 1, the bands of the DNA origami structures are located above the bands of the scaffolds. This indicates that KIT-D is formed in the 2 hours protocol. In addition, the dimer band is clearly visible in the Na⁺-only sample, while the dimer band is thin or absent in the Mg²⁺-containing sample. This suggests that under monovalent salt conditions, it takes longer for the DNA to fold, during which time it may bind to different structures. Furthermore, aggregation is more common at concentrated concentrations of Mg²⁺ only, especially at 22.5 and 25 mM. From this, it can be considered that if the salt concentration is too concentrated, the structures would aggregate and the yield would be poor. Under the 2 hours annealing condition, there is a lot of smear overall, but this is because the purification protocol was not established and a method for clean purification was not known. In the annealing method of protocol 2, a comparison of various prolonged annealing conditions with 17 hours of annealing was performed. As in protocol 1, the dimer band is clearly visible in the Na²⁺-only condition, but in this annealing method, the dimer band is more clearly visible even when annealing with Mg²⁺ is included. This may be attributed to the fact that more dimers are formed over time due to the increased time spent on annealing; more dimers were formed by increasing the time in the 2 hours protocol despite the same salt concentration, indicating that the 2 hours protocol did not result in a ring shape, but rather a relatively large number of DNA nanostructures in an unfolded state DNA nanostructures were relatively abundant in the 2h protocol. Aggregation also appears to increase in all conditions, regardless of salt concentration, compared to 2 hours. This may be due to the fact that, as with the dimer bands, the annealing process over time facilitated the separation of DNA nanostructures that did not form well. The 17 hours protocol, like the 2 hours protocol, also shows a lot of smears and extra staple strands, but this is because the purification method was not working well at this time. The sample was observed and a ring-shaped DNA origami structure was successfully identified. The inner diameter of KIT-D without latches in the open state, is about 40 nm by calculation, and the inner diameter of the DNA nanostructure on the photograph is also about 40 nm. Since this structure has six planes, it can be well assumed to be derived from KIT-D, which is the target of the study. In addition, at this salt concentration, many of the structures were found to be banded with a total length of about 140 nm and a width of about 20 nm, suggesting that they were off-hinged. By comparing each salt concentration condition of the 2h and 17h protocols, it was common that the aggregation increased when the salt concentration was too high. Given that there is room for improvement in the purification method, it was difficult to determine the optimal salt concentration from these results, so it was necessary to try different salt concentration conditions for the latchable structure. Focusing on the borderline where no guirigi-aggregation occurred at each salt concentration, we were able to narrow the conditions to Na⁺ 17.5 M, Mg²⁺ 20 mM, Mg²⁺ 22.5 mM & Na 100 mM salt concentrations for the structure with latch.

Comparison of ring-shaped and band-shaped structures

To compare the difference between open and closed structures, TEM imaging was used to compare samples with open and closed states. Since the AGE result did not show significant difference between samples using a mix of Mg and Na, concentration of Mg 22.5 mM was selected. The results are shown in figure 8.

Figure 8. TEM image comparing open and closed structures

(a)(left)sample of Mg 22.5 mM and Na 100 mM, open with latch 21 nt long,
(b)(right)sample of Mg 22.5 mM and Na 100 mM, closed with latch 21 nt long

From Figure 7, it can be suggested that it is likely to form aggregations in lower salt concentrations and with low and high salt concentrations, bold dimer bands can be observed. Also, bands of closed structures are located slightly lower than the open structures in most samples, suggesting that closed structures have smaller structural size. This is an ideal result for KIT-D, since when the structure is in its closed state, it is assumed for the structure to fold in, resulting in smaller structural size. From Figure 8, there is clearly less aggregation with the closed structures, as well as structures forming dimers. It appears that closed structures have similar size overall, suggesting the possibility of successful KIT-D transformation. However, further analysis and verification is required for clearer and reproducible results.
